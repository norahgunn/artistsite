NORAH GUNN

Statement

My work is a reaction to my anxieties about taking up space: physical, public, domestic, gendered, intellectual, interpersonal, etc. My work uses out-dated and overly specific modes of communication and text to create objects, spaces, and actions that use the language of defeat, incapability, and ineptitude. Many of my main interests fall within the realm of social contracts and mis/communication as a way to investigate class, accessibility, and gender. I use an appropriated masculine blue collar aesthetic measured by overly stereotyped feminine processes and materials that are both informed by my upbringing in Eastern New Jersey and personal experiences as a means to consider the intersection of these things.

b. New Jersey, 1995
Currently lives and works in Philadelphia
